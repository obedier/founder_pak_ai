# AiPay Home — Product Overview

## Overview

AiPay Home is a convenience OS for household shopping. It learns what a household buys, follows explicit user policies, compares across trusted stores, and progressively earns the right to take shopping off the user’s plate.

AiPay Home is not a retailer. It does not own inventory, warehouse goods, or aim to become merchant of record. Instead, it sits above existing merchants and delivery rails and becomes the user’s trusted layer for shopping intent, decisioning, delegation, and control.

## Core Promise

- Shopping disappears.
- Control stays with you.
- Use the stores you already trust, just better.

## Product Thesis

The winning agentic shopping product will not be a chatbot with product links. It will be a trust and execution layer that knows:

- when to recommend
- when to ask
- when to act
- when to stop
- when to undo

## Product Modes

### Explore
The user is uncertain and wants help comparing and understanding options.

Examples:
- Find a good air fryer under $200
- Best healthy snacks for my kids
- Need a new office chair

System behavior:
- compare products
- explain tradeoffs
- shortlist good options
- learn from reactions
- do not purchase autonomously

### Delegate
The user gives an outcome and wants the system to do most of the work.

Examples:
- Buy me a new phone
- Restock this week’s household basics
- Get more of the detergent we usually buy

System behavior:
- infer preferences from memory
- ask only missing questions
- prepare purchase plan or cart
- execute within policy if allowed
- escalate if outside policy or confidence bounds

### Auto
The system proactively buys within narrow, trusted domains.

Examples:
- paper towels
- pantry staples
- pet food
- vitamins
- recurring pharmacy basics

System behavior:
- detect likely need
- compare across allowed merchants
- execute if trust and policy thresholds are met
- notify user
- expose cancel, modify, and feedback controls

## Launch Wedge

The initial wedge is the household operator managing recurring home shopping.

This is usually, but not always, mom. The key is not gender but responsibility. This is the person who carries the invisible operating load for the home:

- staples
- recurring groceries
- pharmacy basics
- kid needs
- replacement items
- speed versus price tradeoffs
- remembering what the household actually prefers

## Why This Wedge

This wedge is attractive because it has:

- high frequency
- obvious pain
- repeatability
- fast trust compounding
- strong retention potential
- word-of-mouth potential

## Product Principles

1. Trust is the product.
2. Magic must be visible.
3. Autonomy is earned, not assumed.
4. Explainability beats black-box cleverness.
5. Relief beats novelty.
6. The product should work with existing stores, not force a new store.
7. Undo and recovery matter as much as execution.
8. Habit should come from better outcomes, not shallow gamification.

## User Value

### Functional value
- less time spent shopping
- fewer repeated decisions
- better price/speed/fit outcomes
- more consistent household memory
- reduced forgotten purchases
- more control over automation

### Emotional value
- this knows me
- this saves me work
- this made a smart choice
- this earned the right to do more

## Core Success Criteria

For the MVP, success means:

- users complete a useful shopping task in the first session or within 24 hours
- users complete a first delegated purchase
- users return weekly for household shopping actions
- at least one category becomes eligible for autopilot
- users describe the product as relief, not just as interesting

## What We Are Not

AiPay Home is not:

- a generic shopping chatbot
- a retailer
- a marketplace with owned inventory
- a coupon site
- an affiliate-content site
- a browser automation gimmick

## Long-Term Company Thesis

The app is the wedge.

The deeper company is the trust and execution layer between consumer intent and merchant fulfillment, including:

- preference memory
- household graph
- policy engine
- trust ledger
- merchant routing
- delegated authorization
- post-purchase recovery
